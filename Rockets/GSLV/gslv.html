GSLV

Geosynchronous Satellite
Launch Vehicle

Basic Information

  • 3-stage launch vehicle

  • Payload capacity:

    6000 kg to LEO
    4000 kg to SSO
    2250 kg to GTO

  • Mass of launch vehicle: 414750 kg

  • Height of launch vehicle: 49.13 m

  • Max Diameter of launch vehicle: 2.8 m

  • First launch on: 18 April 2001

  • Total launches: 15 (9 successes, 2 partial failure and 4 failures)

Stage Information

Side Boosters (L40):

- L40 Liquid propellant stage
- Propellant - UDMH (fuel) and N2O4 (oxidiser)
- Engine - 4x Vikas
- Height - 19.7
- Diameter - 2.1m
- Thrust - 760kN each
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 262 sec
- Burn time - 154 sec
- Propellant mass: 42700 kg each

First stage/Core stage (S139):

- S139 Solid Rocket Motor
- Propellant - HTPB (Hydroxyl-terminated Polybutadiene)
- Height - 20m
- Diameter - 2.8m
- Thrust - 4846.9kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 237 sec (at sea level), 269 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 100 sec
- Propellant mass: 138200 kg

Second Stage (GS2):

- GL40 Liquid propellant stage
- Propellant - UDMH (fuel) and N2O4 (oxidiser)
- Engine - 1x Vikas
- Height - 11.6
- Diameter - 2.8m
- Thrust - 846kN each
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 295 sec
- Burn time - 139 sec
- Propellant mass: 39500 kg each

Third Stage (CUS15):

- Indigenous Cryogenic upper stage
- Propellants - Hydrogen (fuel) and Oxygen (oxidiser)
- Engine - 1x CE-7.5
- Height - 9.9m
- Diameter - 2.8m
- Thrust - 75kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 454 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 846 sec
- Propellant mass: 15000 kg
