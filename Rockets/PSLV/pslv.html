PSLV

Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle

Basic Information

  • 4-stage launch vehicle

  • Payload capacity (XL variant):

    3800 kg to LEO
    1750 kg to SSO
    1300 kg to GTO

  • Variants:

    G (Generic) - Retired
    CA (Core Alone)
    DL (2 boosters)
    QL (4 boosters)
    XL (6 boosters)

  • Mass of launch vehicle (XL variant): 320000 kg

  • Height of launch vehicle: 44m

  • Max Diameter of launch vehicle: 2.8m

  • First launch on: 20 September 1993

  • Total launches: 57 (54 successes, 2 failures and 1 partial failure)

Stage Information

Side Boosters (PSOM-XL):

- S12 Solid Rocket Motor
- Propellant - HTPB (Hydroxyl-terminated Polybutadiene)
- Height - 12m
- Diameter - 1m
- Thrust - 703.5kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 262 sec
- Burn time - 70 sec
- Propellant mass: 12200 kg
- 6x on XL, 4x on QL, 2x on DL, none on CA. (G variant had 6x PSOM - S9 non XL version)

First stage/Core stage (PS1):

- S139 Solid Rocket Motor
- Propellant - HTPB (Hydroxyl-terminated Polybutadiene)
- Height - 20m
- Diameter - 2.8m
- Thrust - 4846.9kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 237 sec (at sea level), 269 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 110 sec
- Propellant mass: 138200 kg

Second stage (PS2):

- Hypergolic liquid propellant stage
- Propellants - UH25 (fuel) and N2O4 (oxidiser)
- Engine - 1x Vikas
- Height - 12.8m
- Diameter - 2.8m
- Thrust - 803.7kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 293 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 133 sec
- Propellant mass: 42000 kg

Third stage (PS3):

- S7 Solid Rocket Motor
- Propellant - HTPB (Hydroxyl-terminated Polybutadiene)
- Height - 3.6m
- Diameter - 2m
- Thrust - 250kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 295 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 113.5 sec
- Propellant mass: 7600 kg

Fourth Stage (PS4):

- Hypergolic liquid propellant stage
- Propellants - MMH (fuel) and MON (oxidiser)
- Engine - 2x ~7.5kN engines
- Height - 3m
- Diameter - 1.3m
- Thrust - 14.66kN
- Specific Impulse (Isp) - 308 sec (in vacuum)
- Burn time - 525 sec
- Propellant mass: 2500 kg max (variable propellant load)
